My Personal Journey

1994

Born :)

2008

Moved to Bangalore (India) in pursuit of better educational opportunities. It was here in Asia's Silicon Valley that I wrote my first line of code, sparking a lifelong fascination with the limitless power of computing.

2011

Started my B.S. in Electrical Engineering, living in a dorm room right on the beach! First encounter with data in an undergrad course and watching Moneyball (starring Brad Pitt) sparked my interest in information theory, programming, and its application in real-world business settings.

2015

Landed my first job as a Data Scientist at Mu Sigma Business Solutions and received my first paycheck! I tackled business problems for Fortune 500 clients using data. The highlight? Seeing my work mentioned in the Wall Street Journal, which fueled my motivation to make a real-world impact.

2017

Invited to join a startup that would go on to shatter industry records. This became the most productive phase of my life, where the fast-paced, high-pressure, and innovation-driven nature of the startup world transformed me. I learned to embrace uncertainty, adapt quickly, and push the boundaries of what I thought was possible.

2020

Amidst the uncertainty of the COVID era, I began my master's degree. While I was already skilled in programming and consulting, I realized that a deeper understanding of the mathematics behind data science was the missing piece in my journey to becoming the best data scientist. This drive to fill that gap pushed me to pursue my master's.

2021

Moved to America, with Florida as the perfect destination for a beach lover like me! This move brought profound changes. I embraced new cultural experiences, developed a deep appreciation for personal freedoms, and rediscovered my love for the beach lifestyle, fostering a renewed sense of balance and independence.

2022

Rehired by TheMathCompany to take on a pivotal role in a new geography, reflecting the trust and confidence earned in previous positions.

2023

Successfully led my first team, solving complex business problems that drove critical product decisions directly affecting millions of consumers. This role marked a significant step in my career, showcasing my leadership and data-driven decision-making abilities. See recommendations.

2024

A year of firsts: made my first Bitcoin transaction, bought my first augmented reality hardware, and built my first generative AI product. Exploring new frontiers, one leap at a time!
